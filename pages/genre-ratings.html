Genre Ratings Analysis

Comparing average ratings across different movie genres

Introduction

This visualization explores how different movie genres are rated by viewers. We analyze rating patterns across genres to identify which categories consistently receive higher ratings and which show the most variability in audience reception.

Power BI Visualization

Rating Distribution by Score (D3.js)

Key Findings

Highest Rated Genres

Documentary, animation, and adventure films consistently receive the highest average ratings across all time periods. These genres maintain strong audience appreciation regardless of changing trends.

Most Polarizing Genres

Horror and thriller genres show the highest rating variance, indicating more polarized audience reactions. These genres tend to have strong niche appeal rather than broad audience satisfaction.

Rating Trends Over Time

Science fiction and superhero films have seen significant increases in average ratings since 2010, reflecting improved production quality and mainstream acceptance of these genres.

Genre Combinations

Films categorized in multiple genres (e.g., drama-comedy, action-adventure) typically receive higher ratings than single-genre films, suggesting that genre-blending appeals to wider audiences.

Methodology Notes

This visualization uses bar charts and box plots to represent rating distributions across genres. Average ratings are calculated using a weighted mean that accounts for the number of votes each film received.

Genre classifications are based on primary and secondary genre tags in the TMDB dataset. Films with multiple genres are counted in each applicable category for the distribution analysis.
