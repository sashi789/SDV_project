Interactive Visualizations

Explore our movie data through interactive charts and graphs

Introduction

This page features a collection of interactive visualizations that allow you to explore different aspects of our movie dataset. Use the controls to filter and customize each visualization to discover insights about movie trends, ratings, and more.

Genre Evolution Over Time (D3.js)

This visualization shows how different movie genres have evolved in popularity over the decades. The stacked area chart displays the proportion of movies released in each genre, allowing you to see shifts in the film industry's focus over time.

Budget vs. Revenue by Genre (Plotly)

Explore the relationship between movie budgets and box office revenues across different genres. This interactive scatter plot allows you to identify which genres tend to provide the best return on investment and how budget size correlates with financial success.

Rating Distribution by Release Year (Vega-Lite)

This heatmap visualization shows the distribution of movie ratings across different release years. The color intensity represents the number of movies that received a particular rating in a given year, allowing you to identify trends in movie quality over time.

Methodology Notes

These visualizations use data from The Movie Database (TMDB) API. The dataset includes information about movies released between 1960 and 2020, including budget, revenue, genre, rating, and release date information.

For the budget vs. revenue analysis, all monetary values have been adjusted for inflation to 2020 dollars to allow for meaningful comparison across different time periods. Movies with missing budget or revenue data have been excluded from the analysis.

The genre evolution chart shows the relative proportion of movies in each genre over time, based on the primary genre classification in the TMDB database. Movies can belong to multiple genres, but only the primary genre is considered in this visualization.
